My research aims to answer the general question of how to tune the size of neural networks *while they are training* . If you think about it, the cost of large neural networks is primarily determined by their size, which depends on architectural choices like the number of layers, layer sizes, and weight structures. [09/01/2025, 21:35:45] Jamie Martin EF: Tips I use/follow/have come up with:

1. Instead of just Asking it to implement something, ask the model to identify best practices for doing so, then to follow said best practices. 

e.g. instead of write a unit test for my code. I’d say:

- what are the best practices for writing unit tests?

Then use that as a template to say “follow these best practices and write a unit test for my code”.

You can apply this to most things, and the results are generally a lot better. 

eg for bolt what a good website design would be. 

It’s not as good as fully copiloting or having an expert do it, but it’s pretty good. 

2. Prompt it to ask you questions for core requirements before answering. 

3. When asking it to fix errors, instead of asking it to fix, ask it to work with you to debug, going one step at a time to avoid all the “if this is the issue do this, if that then do that”. This one has worked every time for me. Can always solve the issue. ‎<This message was edited>
